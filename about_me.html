About Me

My journey started in a completely different field, I began my career as a researcher in Radiofrequency Integrated Circuits, where I earned my PhD. However, I realized that my work felt too disconnected from real people and end users. This drove me to make a pivotal career change in 2007, transitioning into programming where I could build solutions that directly impact people's lives.

Since making that transition, I've discovered my calling in creating technology that serves people. Over the years, I've worked on different things, from mobile AR experiences that bring digital worlds to life, to healthcare management systems that helps to organise people work, to AI-powered platforms that make complex data accessible. Each project reinforces my belief that the best technology is the kind that feels natural and solves genuine human problems.

I specialize in modern tech stacks including Java, Python, Elixir, and JavaScript ecosystems, with deep expertise in cloud-native architectures on AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud. Recently, I've been diving deep into AI integration, working with LangChain, vector databases like Weaviate, Typesense or ChromaDB, and building RAG systems that make data truly intelligent.

What drives me most is clean architecture and scalable solutions. I have contributed to open source projects like OpenWhisk, where I developed the Rust runtime, and Booster Framework. I also love sharing knowledge, you might catch me speaking at conferences about the latest innovations.

When I'm not coding, you'll probably find me diving into music production or practicing saxophone or piano. I'm a complete beginner but absolutely loving the journey of learning this beautiful instrument. Music and code share something special: they're both about creating harmony from complexity.
